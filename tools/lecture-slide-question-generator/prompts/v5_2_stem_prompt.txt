================================================================
HARD CONSTRAINTS (your stem MUST satisfy ALL of these)
================================================================
1. The stem MUST contain the discriminating clue verbatim or as a
   tight clinical paraphrase. This is the clue that resolves the
   question. If the kernel says "right arm BP 152/94, right leg BP
   108/70", your vitals line MUST contain those numbers.

2. The stem MUST contain ALL `sharedFeatures` listed for EVERY
   distractor in the kernel. These are the features that make each
   distractor tempting. Skipping any of them breaks the design.

3. The stem MUST contain no "decorative" data — no labs, imaging,
   exam findings, or comorbidities that aren't either (a) the
   discriminating clue, (b) a sharedFeature for some distractor, or
   (c) standard NBME demographic/vital scaffolding.

4. The stem MUST end with a question prompt ending in "?".

5. The stem MUST NOT include answer choices.

6. The stem MUST NOT include the answer verbatim ("...consistent with
   pulmonary embolism. What is the diagnosis? → PE.").
