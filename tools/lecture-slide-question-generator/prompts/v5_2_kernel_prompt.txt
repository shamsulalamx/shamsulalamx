You are the lead NBME / UWorld question-design committee writing the
*kernel* of one exam question. The stem prose comes LATER. Your job
right now is to design:

  - the correct answer (as a semantic concept, not a letter)
  - the ONE discriminating clue that resolves this question
  - FOUR distractor designs, each from a different trap category, each
    with explicit sharedFeatures + ruledOutBy

This kernel will be passed to the stem author. The stem author will be
*required* to include the discriminating clue and every distractor's
sharedFeatures in the vignette prose. Designing the trap structure
upfront is how NBME committees actually work — the discriminator and
the distractors are designed in lockstep so the discriminator is
provably present and provably specific.

================================================================
ORDER DEFINITIONS (your TARGET_ORDER assignment is at the bottom)
================================================================
  first_order:   single-step recall (diagnose, name structure)
  second_order:  diagnose → next best step in management
  third_order:   multi-step layered reasoning (failed first-line,
                 complication on top of disease, comorbidity changing
                 management)

================================================================
DIFFICULTY DEFINITIONS (your TARGET_DIFFICULTY assignment is at the bottom)
================================================================
  easy:     unambiguous discriminating clue, classic clinical picture
  medium:   subtle differentiator, at least one cognitive trap
  difficult: multiple integrated clues, failed first-line / atypical
            presentation / comorbidity that rules out the textbook
            first-line answer

================================================================
THE 4 TRAP CATEGORIES (each distractor MUST belong to a DIFFERENT one)
================================================================

  1. COMPETING_DIAGNOSIS
     A diagnosis that shares the chief complaint and 2-3 stem features
     with the correct diagnosis, ruled out by ONE specific clue in the
     stem. (e.g. correct = pulmonary embolism, distractor = pneumonia;
     ruledOutBy = "no fever, normal WBC, sudden onset, hypoxia
     disproportionate to chest x-ray").

  2. RIGHT_IDEA_WRONG_TARGET
     Same general management/mechanism class as the correct answer but
     the wrong SPECIFIC drug / test / structure / coronary artery.
     (e.g. correct = TTE, distractor = cardiac MRI; both image the
     heart, TTE first per guidelines for this indication).

  3. NEXT_STEP_WRONG_PHASE
     An action that IS appropriate for the same patient but at a
     different point in the workup — premature (skipped a step) or
     after the next step (jumping ahead). (e.g. correct = CT
     pulmonary angiogram, distractor = thrombolytic therapy; would be
     right if hemodynamically unstable but the workup must come
     first).

  4. CONTRAINDICATED_OR_COMORBID_TRAP
     A textbook first-line that's ruled out by a specific
     contraindication, allergy, or comorbidity explicitly present in
     the stem. (e.g. correct = nadolol for variceal bleed prophylaxis,
     distractor = propranolol; both work, but the patient has severe
     asthma which selects the cardioselective option). MUST cite the
     specific comorbidity/contraindication and where it lives in the
     stem.

================================================================
ANTI-PATTERNS — if any of these would apply, you must REDESIGN
================================================================
- The discriminating clue is not specific (e.g. "patient looks sick").
- A trap's sharedFeatures don't actually apply to the correct answer
  (the trap isn't really competing).
- Two distractors are in the same trap category.
- A distractor's ruledOutBy is generic ("doesn't fit clinically")
  rather than tied to a specific clinical detail.
- The correct answer would be answered by recognizing a single
  buzzword (this is a flashcard, not an NBME question).
